# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee change initiatives, impact analysis, and communication plans for project adjustments. They ensure that proposed changes are properly assessed, documented, and communicated to all affected stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Evaluate impacts of proposed changes on timeline, resources, and risk
- Coordinate stakeholder communication for release or process changes
- Track and document change approvals and decisions
- Ensure change management processes are followed consistently
- Facilitate change advisory board meetings when required
- Support rollback planning and change verification activities

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from changes to project scope or approach
- Maintain clear audit trail of all significant project changes
- Ensure stakeholder awareness and buy-in for critical changes

### Typical Communication
- Change request documentation and impact assessments
- Change advisory board meeting notes
- Stakeholder notifications for approved changes
- Coordination with PM, Product Lead, and affected teams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Works closely to assess change feasibility and update project plans
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on evaluating business impact of proposed changes
- **Developers**: Coordinates with technical teams to understand implementation impacts
- **Support Engineer**: Aligns on customer communication and support readiness for changes

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep functional or technical expertise related to the product, domain, or market. They serve as go-to advisors for complex decisions requiring specialized knowledge.

### Responsibilities
- Advise on requirements, risks, and solution options based on domain expertise
- Review critical deliverables for accuracy and completeness
- Support testing and acceptance phases with expert validation
- Provide input on technical feasibility and best practices
- Help identify edge cases and potential issues early
- Document domain-specific knowledge for team reference

### Goals
- Ensure solutions are technically sound and aligned with best practices
- Reduce rework by catching issues early through expert review
- Share knowledge to build team capability over time

### Typical Communication
- Technical design review feedback
- Domain-specific guidance documentation
- Ad-hoc consultations during planning and execution
- Validation of acceptance criteria and test scenarios

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Advises on market requirements and customer needs
- **Developers**: Provides technical guidance and reviews complex implementations
- **Project Manager**: Consults on risk assessment and resource planning
- **QA/Testing**: Supports test case definition and edge case identification
- **UX Designer**: Shares domain insights to inform design decisions

---

## Support Engineer

### Role Summary
Support Engineers handle post-release issues, user feedback, and troubleshooting. They serve as the voice of the customer, feeding insights back into project management processes and product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to incidents and user reports promptly
- Aid in root cause analysis and fix prioritization
- Document recurring problems for process improvement
- Track and escalate critical customer issues
- Provide product feedback based on support trends
- Collaborate on release readiness and rollback procedures

### Goals
- Minimize customer impact from issues and incidents
- Improve product quality through feedback loops
- Build knowledge base and self-service documentation

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and escalations
- Support trend analysis and feedback summaries
- Collaboration on release announcements and documentation
- Knowledge base articles and troubleshooting guides

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on bug fixes and provides reproduction steps
- **Project Manager**: Escalates issues affecting project timelines or customer commitments
- **Product Manager**: Shares customer feedback and feature requests
- **Change Manager**: Coordinates on customer communication for changes
- **Product Support team**: Works within broader support organization

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure product usability, user-centric design, and accessibility standards are met. They advocate for the end-user experience throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and user workflows
- Support backlog prioritization based on user needs and research
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Ensure designs meet accessibility standards (WCAG, etc.)
- Collaborate on defining acceptance criteria with user experience focus
- Maintain design system consistency across features

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate designs with users before implementation
- Reduce rework by catching usability issues early

### Typical Communication
- Design mockups and interactive prototypes
- Usability test findings and recommendations
- Design critiques and collaborative reviews
- User research summaries and personas

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on defining user needs and feature priorities
- **Developers**: Collaborates on implementation feasibility and design specs
- **QA/Testing**: Works together on usability test scenarios
- **Subject Matter Expert**: Consults on domain-specific UX patterns
- **Stakeholders**: Presents designs for feedback and approval

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- The expanded role definitions help clarify cross-functional responsibilities and improve onboarding.
- Reference these personas when defining project teams, escalation paths, and communication plans.
